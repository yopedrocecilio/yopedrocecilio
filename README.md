<br/>

I grew up in Brazil watching brilliant people get ignored.

Not because they lacked talent.  
Because they lacked proximity.

Wrong passport. Wrong city. Wrong school name.

The world kept optimizing for where you started.  
Not where you could go.

That felt like a bug.  
I decided to spend my life fixing it.

<div align="center">

### The shift nobody talks about

</div>

We are living through the last era where geography decides destiny.

AGI doesn't care where you were born.  
A model doesn't check your passport.  
A tool doesn't ask what city you live in.

For the first time in history —  
the gap between a kid in Lagos and a kid in San Francisco  
is closing faster than any policy, any institution, any government ever managed.

That is the most important thing happening right now.  
Most people are distracted by the noise.

<br/>

> *"Any sufficiently advanced technology is indistinguishable from magic."*  
> <sub>— Arthur C. Clarke</sub>

The magic is real. The question is who gets to use it.

<div align="center">

### On work. After AGI.

</div>

For most of human history, work meant survival.  
Then it meant identity.  
Then it meant status.

Now we have to ask a harder question:

**What is work for — when machines can do most of it?**

I don't think the answer is leisure.  
I think the answer is meaning.

People don't want to stop working.  
They want to stop working on things that don't matter.

AGI won't take jobs.  
It will expose which jobs were never worth doing.

The builders who survive won't be the ones who code fastest.  
They'll be the ones who know *why* they're building.

<br/>

```
curiosity  →  the only skill that doesn't deprecate
judgment   →  what models still can't buy
taste      →  rarer than intelligence
care       →  the last human edge
```
